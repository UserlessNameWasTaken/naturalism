Hamlin Garland focused on the struggles of rural life in the American Midwest, emphasizing realism and hardship. His collection Main-Travelled Roads portrays farmers facing economic difficulty and physical exhaustion. Garland believed that environment and living conditions strongly influenced behavior and opportunity. His writing rejects romanticized views of rural life, instead presenting it as harsh and limiting. He was influenced by Naturalist ideas about determinism and social forces. Garland’s work highlights how geography and economic conditions shape human experience.

Hamlin Garland concentrated his attention on describing the difficulties faced by farmers in the Midwest region of America. The collection of Hamlin Garland titled “Main-Travelled Roads” describes farmers experiencing financial hardships and extreme tiredness. According to Hamlin Garland, environmental factors had an impact on the character and opportunities of individuals. Hamlin Garland does not paint an idyllic picture of the hardships associated with rural areas but rather presents it in a negative and constraining way.

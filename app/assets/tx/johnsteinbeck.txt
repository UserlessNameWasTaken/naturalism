John Steinbeck’s writings contain a lot of elements of Naturalism, especially in his depiction of economic struggles and the natural environment. In the novel “The Grapes of Wrath,” he illustrates the story of those forced to leave their homes because of the Depression and the Dust Bowl. Steinbeck’s heroes fight with various forces, including poverty, the environment, and oppression. Steinbeck shows both strength and weaknesses in people and believes that an individual can only do so much.

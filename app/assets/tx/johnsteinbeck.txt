John Steinbeck’s work reflects strong Naturalist themes, particularly in its focus on economic hardship and environmental conditions. In The Grapes of Wrath, he portrays families displaced by the Great Depression and Dust Bowl. His characters struggle against forces like poverty, nature, and systemic inequality. Steinbeck emphasizes the resilience of individuals while acknowledging the limits of their control. His writing combines emotional depth with social critique. He is one of the most influential American authors connected to Naturalism.

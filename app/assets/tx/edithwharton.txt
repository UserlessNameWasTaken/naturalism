Wharton looks into the impacts of social systems and class dynamics on individuals. For example, in The House of Mirth, Wharton considers the restrictions placed on women in high-class societies, thus demonstrating how the environment influences one’s ability to make decisions. Ethan Frome provides a rather gloomy depiction of the life of rural people, who find themselves prisoners of their own surroundings. Wharton’s characters generally find themselves in situations that they cannot control; instead, they depend on their social statuses.

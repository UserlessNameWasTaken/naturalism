Edith Wharton explores how social structures and class expectations shape individual lives. In The House of Mirth, she examines the constraints placed on women in high society, showing how environment limits personal freedom. Ethan Frome presents a bleak portrayal of rural life, where isolation and circumstance trap the characters. Wharton’s work often highlights the tension between individual desire and societal pressure. Her characters frequently face outcomes determined by their social position rather than their choices. This focus aligns her with Naturalist themes of determinism and environmental influence.

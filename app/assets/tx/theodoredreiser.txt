The novels of Theodore Dreiser explore the influence of social and economic factors on the ambitions and morals of individuals. Sister Carrie depicts the life of an aspiring young lady who faces the challenges of city life through the lens of her desires and necessities rather than her moral values. The author’s protagonists often confront systems that are not in their control, in accordance with the deterministic principles of Naturalism. The author employs a straightforward and bare literary language to emphasize the realistic nature of his themes.

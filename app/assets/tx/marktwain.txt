Mark Twain is one of the major figures associated with American Realism, the style that paved the way for Naturalism. The depiction of society in Adventures of Huckleberry Finn by Mark Twain is realistic, and it includes the problems of race and morality. Social criticism is one of Twain's favorite themes, and he pays a lot of attention to the impact of environment on people's actions. Mark Twain frequently employs the vernacular dialect when creating his characters. He is not a strict follower of the Naturalistic school; however, he belongs to its spirit.

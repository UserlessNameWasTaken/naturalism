Stephan Crane is an important literary figure in the Naturalist movement in America, famous for his strong, psychologically-based stories. His story The Red Badge of Courage discusses the psychological and instinctual side of a soldier involved in battle, stressing the effect of pressure on people. In his previous work Maggie: A Girl of the Streets, he described poverty and life in an urban environment and stressed the certainty of being trapped within a social class. It is clear from the works of Stephan Crane that a person is helpless against external influences such as wars, societies, and nature.

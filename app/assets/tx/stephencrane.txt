Stephen Crane is a key American Naturalist known for his intense and psychologically driven narratives. In The Red Badge of Courage, he explores the internal fears and instincts of a soldier during war, emphasizing how individuals react under extreme pressure. His earlier work, Maggie: A Girl of the Streets, portrays urban poverty and the inevitability of social downfall. Crane’s writing suggests that humans are often powerless against external forces like war, society, and environment. He avoids romanticizing his subjects, instead presenting raw and often brutal realities. His work reflects Naturalism’s focus on determinism and the limits of human agency.

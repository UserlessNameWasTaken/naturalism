Survival, instinct, and the interplay between man and nature have always been Jack London’s themes of interest. In The Call of the Wild and White Fang, London shows how man’s instinctive nature affects his decisions and actions through animals who serve as protagonists in the novels. His literature demonstrates that survival is not easy for those in tough natural conditions, and only the strongest and most instinctive survive. Environment is considered an essential part of character development in London’s literature. Civilization is shown as something ephemeral compared to primitive instincts.

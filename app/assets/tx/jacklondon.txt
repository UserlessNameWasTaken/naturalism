Jack London is known for his exploration of survival, instinct, and the relationship between humans and nature. In The Call of the Wild and White Fang, he uses animal protagonists to illustrate Naturalist ideas about instinct and adaptation. His stories emphasize the struggle for survival in harsh environments, where strength and instinct often determine outcomes. London’s work reflects the belief that environment plays a crucial role in shaping behavior. He often portrays civilization as a thin layer over more primal instincts. His writing connects Naturalism with themes of evolution and the power of nature.

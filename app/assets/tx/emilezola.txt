Émile Zola is considered the pioneer and the main theorist of the literary movement called Naturalism. Zola thought that people's actions were dictated by their inheritance and environment. The novelist viewed his works as social experiments. Through the Rougon-Macquart cycle, he described how inheritance and surrounding conditions affected people through generations. Novels such as Germinal show the truth about the life of coal miners, with emphasis on the influence of the environment. People had little or no power over their destiny according to Zola's novels.

Frank Norris played a major role in shaping American Naturalism by blending realism with broader social critique. His novel McTeague depicts the gradual moral decline of its protagonist, influenced by greed and circumstance. In The Octopus, Norris explores the power of corporations and the struggles of individuals against economic systems. He was particularly interested in how larger forces, such as capitalism, control human lives. His writing often portrays characters as driven by instinct rather than rational decision-making. Norris helped expand Naturalism to include critiques of industrial society.

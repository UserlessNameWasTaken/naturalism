Norris contributed significantly to the formation of Naturalism in America through the combination of realistic portrayal with social criticism. His novel, McTeague, describes the steady deterioration of the main character’s morals due to greed and circumstances. The novel, The Octopus, illustrates the force and power that corporate bodies have and how individuals struggle against economic systems. Norris was highly concerned with the way in which bigger powers, like capitalism, controlled the lives of people. His works usually feature characters who act on impulse and not on reason.

Henry James is primarily associated with realism, but his focus on psychological depth influenced Naturalist writers. His works, such as The Portrait of a Lady, explore how social environments shape individual identity. James is known for examining the inner thoughts and motivations of his characters. While less deterministic than strict Naturalists, his work still emphasizes the impact of external pressures. He often portrays characters navigating complex social systems. His influence helped expand the psychological dimension of Naturalist literature.

Richard Wright explores the impact of race, environment, and systemic oppression on individual identity. In Native Son, he presents a protagonist shaped by poverty and discrimination. Wright’s work reflects Naturalist ideas by emphasizing how external forces limit personal freedom. His writing is direct and often unsettling, highlighting harsh social realities. He focuses on how society constructs barriers that individuals cannot easily escape. Wright’s contributions expand Naturalism to address issues of race and inequality in modern America.

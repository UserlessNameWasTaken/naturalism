Kate Chopin is well known for her examination of identity, gender roles, and individual freedoms in the restrictive societies in which her characters find themselves. In The Awakening, Chopin portrays the challenges faced by a woman who rebels against the conventions of society and her quest for self-sufficiency. Many of Chopin’s characters face external pressures from social convention as well as their own internal conflicts. She follows the naturalist approach to a certain degree by examining the effects of both environment and culture on the individual.

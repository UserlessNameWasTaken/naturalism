Kate Chopin is known for her exploration of identity, gender, and personal freedom within restrictive environments. In The Awakening, she examines a woman’s struggle against societal expectations and her search for independence. Chopin’s characters are often shaped by social norms and internal desires that conflict with those norms. Her work reflects Naturalist ideas by showing how environment and culture influence behavior. She also incorporates psychological realism, exploring characters’ inner lives in depth. Chopin’s writing bridges Naturalism and early feminist thought.
